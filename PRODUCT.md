# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Individual Ethereum Security round donors are the primary users. They use the allocator to compare many projects, tune priorities, and build a ballot they can defend. Ecosystem stewards and researchers are a secondary audience who use the same surface to review the recommendations and methodology.

## Product Purpose

How To Vote turns live round metadata and a local curation layer into a recommended ballot split. The product should help users move from uncertainty to a confident allocation quickly, while keeping the scoring logic legible enough to trust without forcing them through long explanations.

## Brand Personality

Analytical, calm, credible, concise. The product should feel like a serious research tool with editorial restraint: deliberate hierarchy, sparse copy, no hype, no motivational filler, and no false friendliness. Mipland is a useful reference for composure and pacing, adapted here for a data-heavy product interface.

## Anti-references

Verbose AI-sounding copy. "This is X, not Y" framing. Generic SaaS gradients and ornamental chrome. Explanation blocks that over-teach obvious actions or repeat headings. Shareability theater that competes with the core decision-making task.

## Design Principles

1. Confidence before flourish.
2. Show judgment quickly, reveal reasoning on demand.
3. Dense information should feel calm, not cramped.
4. Copy must be specific, sparse, and useful.
5. Every control should make ballot tuning easier.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Respect reduced motion. Treat desktop as the primary working context, with solid mobile support. Prioritize clear labels, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, and contrast that supports long analytical sessions.
