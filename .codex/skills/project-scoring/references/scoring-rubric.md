Use this rubric unless the repo input artifacts provide a stricter variant.

## Dimensions

- `trackRecord`: Evidence of shipped work, adoption, continuity, and time in market.
- `underfundedness`: Whether marginal funding likely matters now. Higher for smaller public-goods, research, or nonprofit efforts; lower for clearly commercial organizations.
- `ecosystemLeverage`: How broadly the work improves Ethereum builders, infrastructure, users, coordination, or security posture.
- `publicGoodsOpenness`: Open-source posture, public-good orientation, and visible public artifacts.
- `executionClarity`: How clearly the team explains the work, roadmap, and expected use of funds.

## Theme keys

Use only these theme keys:

- `core-protocol-client-security`
- `tooling-fuzzing-formal-verification`
- `monitoring-incident-response-ops`
- `wallet-app-user-safety`
- `cryptography-zk-security`
- `education-research-coordination`

## Track Record

- `1`: Project is under 2 months old, pre-launch, hackathon-grade, or only a concept/demo.
- `2`: Project is at least 2 months old but under 1 year old, with an initial shipped artifact and limited continuity evidence.
- `3`: Project is at least 1 year old but under 2 years old, or age is unclear but there is moderate evidence of shipped work and some continuity.
- `4`: Project is at least 2 years old but under 4 years old, with repeated maintenance, releases, or integrations.
- `5`: Project is over 4 years old and still maintained, with strong evidence of real-world usage, repeated releases, or production deployment.

Modifiers:

- Cap at `2` if the project still looks like a prototype or one-off demo.
- Cap at `3` if the project is old but appears inactive.
- Use explicit age claims, release history, public usage, integrations, or long-running maintenance as evidence.

## Underfundedness

- `1`: Clearly well-capitalized or commercially funded. Examples: enterprise product, clear revenue model, large foundation budget, or obvious non-donation funding depth.
- `2`: Some evidence of existing commercial or institutional funding, but donations could still help at the margin.
- `3`: Mixed case. Independent or public-interest work exists, but funding urgency, runway, or marginal impact is not explicit.
- `4`: Small-team, public-goods, research, or nonprofit effort where donations clearly extend maintenance, research, or delivery.
- `5`: Marginal donations directly buy continued operation in a measurable way. Examples: explicit month-of-runway math, volunteer-to-paid transition, or single-maintainer critical infrastructure with little alternate funding.

Evidence to weigh:

- Team size and independence
- Nonprofit/public-goods/research positioning
- Explicit use-of-funds language
- Commercial revenue, services, or enterprise sales
- Whether the project appears already financed without this round

## Ecosystem Leverage

- `1`: Benefits one project, one internal team, or a very narrow local workflow.
- `2`: Helps a niche segment or a small number of external users, with limited downstream reuse.
- `3`: Reusable by multiple teams or materially helpful to one meaningful Ethereum security segment.
- `4`: Broadly reusable across many protocols, developers, researchers, wallets, or infrastructure operators.
- `5`: Foundational security infrastructure, standard-setting work, or upstream tooling/research whose benefits compound across large parts of Ethereum.

Evidence to weigh:

- Number and diversity of downstream users
- Whether the work sits upstream in the stack
- Whether it improves security posture beyond a single product
- Whether other tools, protocols, or teams can build on it directly

## Public Goods Openness

- `1`: Closed source or largely private deliverables, with little public artifact value.
- `2`: Some public artifacts exist, but key outputs are closed, hard to reuse, or access is materially restricted.
- `3`: Public outputs exist and are usable, but openness is mixed, incomplete, or poorly evidenced.
- `4`: Clearly open-source or public-facing work with reusable repositories, docs, research, or datasets.
- `5`: Strong public-goods posture with open, reusable artifacts, transparent maintenance, and obvious value to people beyond the sponsor or core team.

Evidence to weigh:

- Public repo and documentation availability
- Reusability of artifacts
- Public-good framing versus private capture
- Visible external access without permissioning

## Execution Clarity

- `1`: The project pitch is vague. It is hard to tell what the team does next or how funds would be used.
- `2`: The project explains what it is, but next steps or funding use remain mostly unspecified.
- `3`: The project clearly explains the work and gives some sense of future work or funding use, but without much detail.
- `4`: The project names concrete upcoming work, deliverables, or funding use with reasonable specificity.
- `5`: The project ties donations to explicit outputs, timelines, or budget math. Examples: milestone plan, quantified deliverables, or "$X funds Y months of work."

## General scoring guidance

- Treat missing evidence as missing, not implicitly positive.
- Use the full scale, but avoid `1` or `5` unless the evidence is clear.
- Keep dimensions independent; do not let one strong attribute inflate the rest.
- When uncertain, stay near `3` and lower confidence rather than over-claiming.
